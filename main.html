    Frequently Asked Questions

    ASCII art is a graphic design technique that uses printable characters from the ASCII standard to create images. Our generator analyzes the brightness values of each pixel in your uploaded image and maps them to corresponding ASCII characters, creating a text-based representation of your image. The denser characters represent darker areas, while lighter characters represent brighter regions.

    Our ASCII Art Generator supports the most common image formats: JPG, JPEG, and PNG files. For optimal results, we recommend using high-contrast images with clear subject matter. The maximum file size is 5MB to ensure fast processing and generation times.

    Character density determines how many characters are used to represent your image horizontally. Low density (80 characters) creates smaller, simpler ASCII art suitable for quick previews. Medium density (120 characters) offers a balanced approach with good detail. High density (160 characters) provides the most detailed output, perfect for complex images with fine details.

    The Invert Colors option reverses the ASCII character mapping, switching dark and light characters. This is useful when you want to display your ASCII art on different backgrounds. For example, if your original image has dark subjects on light backgrounds, inverting will make it appear correctly on dark backgrounds.

Images with too many fine details may become unclear at lower densities, so experiment with different density settings. Consider the final display medium when choosing whether to invert colors.
